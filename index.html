Mt. Everest

Mount Everest, Earth's tallest peak, stands at 29,032 feet (8,848 meters) above sea level. Located in the Himalayas on the border between Nepal and China, it attracts adventurers worldwide. Climbing it is treacherous, with extreme weather, altitude sickness, and avalanches posing constant threats. Yet, it remains an iconic symbol of human endurance.

Why mountains are necessary?

Mountains are essential for ecological balance, serving as watersheds, regulating climate, and fostering biodiversity. They provide habitats for diverse flora and fauna, protect against erosion, and offer resources like minerals, water, and recreational opportunities. Culturally and spiritually, they inspire awe, offering solace and connection to nature.
